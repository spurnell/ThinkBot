In February 2026, NIST's [Center for AI Standards and Innovation](https://www.nist.gov/caisi) quietly launched something consequential: the [AI Agent Standards Initiative](https://www.nist.gov/news-events/news/2026/02/announcing-ai-agent-standards-initiative-interoperable-and-secure), a coordinated effort to develop interoperability, security, and governance frameworks for autonomous AI systems. The initiative is racing to establish mutual recognition mechanisms with the EU's ENISA, Japan's AIST, and [ISO/IEC JTC 1/SC 42](https://www.iso.org/committee/6794475.html) — the international standards committee where the technical architecture of AI is being negotiated, one working group at a time — by 2027.

That deadline is not a bureaucratic milestone. It is an inflection point. The window to establish American design authority over the global architecture of agentic AI is open. It will not stay open long. And right now, the US is showing up to a geopolitical standards contest with a chronically underfunded technical agency and no clear Congressional mandate to resource it properly.

That needs to change.
