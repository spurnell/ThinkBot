The world's longest-running truce in digital trade is over. After 28 years of continuous renewal, the WTO's moratorium on customs duties for electronic transmissions [expired without successor rules](https://theasiagroup.com/wto-e-commerce-moratorium-expires-without-renewal/) at the 14th Ministerial Conference in Yaoundé, Cameroon this past March — the first time since 1998 that WTO members have been legally free to impose tariffs on digital goods and services. Almost simultaneously, Bloomberg asked the question that Washington has been quietly dreading: ["Is the World Finally Going to Tariff Big Tech?"](https://www.bloomberg.com/opinion/articles/2026-04-09/is-the-world-finally-going-to-tariff-big-tech)
