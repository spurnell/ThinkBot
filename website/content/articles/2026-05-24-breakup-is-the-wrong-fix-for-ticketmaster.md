**Accuracy fixes**

1. **Ford Motor Co. quote removed.** The specific quote "carefully fashioned so as to cure only the specific harms found" could not be verified as appearing in *Ford Motor Co. v. United States*, 405 U.S. 562. The passage was replaced with a paraphrase of the genuine doctrinal principle, now citing the DOJ's own Section 2 report (Chapter 9) as the authoritative source.

2. **Microsoft case description corrected.** The draft claimed the D.C. Circuit "held that the district court had failed to connect the structural remedy to the specific violations found." The revised paragraph describes the actual holding accurately while preserving the article's point.

Per Crowell & Moring's post-verdict analysis, defendants disputed the 86% figure at trial with a 44% counter-figure. Both figures now carry the qualification "as alleged in the states' complaint — and credited by the jury's liability finding."
