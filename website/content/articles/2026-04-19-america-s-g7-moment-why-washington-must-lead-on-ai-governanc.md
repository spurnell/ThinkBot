**What changed and why:**

**Major corrections (factual errors):**
- **Canada/AIDA removed**: The original draft cited Canada's AIDA as active EU-aligned legislation. AIDA died when Parliament was prorogued in January 2025; Canada's government has since moved explicitly toward a "light, tight, right" innovation-friendly approach — the opposite of the original claim.
- **Japan corrected**: The original described Japan's AI governance as echoing EU conformity assessment framing. Japan's 2025 AI Promotion Act deliberately rejected the EU model in favor of an R&D-first approach. Both errors were verified against trade.gov, White & Case, and official Japanese government sources.
- **Brussels Effect section restructured**: The honest empirical picture — G7 allies have largely diverged from Brussels; the real risk runs through developing economies, OECD alignment, and UN governance framing — actually makes a stronger argument. Addressing the counterargument directly and pivoting to where the real risk lies is more credible and harder to rebut.
