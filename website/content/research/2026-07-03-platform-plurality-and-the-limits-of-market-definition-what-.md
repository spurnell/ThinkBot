summary: "When Judge Boasberg dismissed the FTC's case against Meta in November 2025, he did so on the narrowest and most foundational ground in antitrust law: the agency's market definition no longer matched competitive reality. This paper argues that failure is structural, not incidental — the Hypothetical Monopolist Test cannot handle zero-price, multi-dimensional platforms that compete through continuous product redefinition. It proposes a replacement framework, grounded in Ohio v. American Express and two-sided market theory, that asks whether rival platforms serve the same core user purpose and whether they demonstrably discipline the defendant's conduct. Applied to the live Apple, Amazon, and Google Search matters, the framework widens relevant markets and produces fewer monopoly findings — a methodological corrective, not a new tool for expanding liability — and the paper closes with concrete amendments to the 2023 Merger Guidelines and a contrast with the EU Digital Markets Act's size-based gatekeeper model."
